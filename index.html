🧩 Next Pandemic Game

Exploring pandemic risk through viral families, spillovers & human behavior

📚 Introduction

This educational project explores which viruses might cause the next human pandemic — and why. Built as an interactive model, it incorporates data on virology, ecology, and behavior.

🦠 High-Risk Viral Families

  • Coronaviruses (CoVs): Fast mutation, history of pandemics, bat origins.
  • Influenza Viruses: Reassortment, antigenic shift, zoonosis from birds and pigs.
  • Paramyxoviruses: Nipah & Hendra — high mortality, bat origins.
  • Filoviruses: Ebola, Marburg — severe outbreaks, limited healthcare infrastructure.
  • Arboviruses: Spread by mosquitoes (Zika, Dengue, Chikungunya), expanding with climate change.
  • Hantaviruses: Rodent-borne, increasing spillover risk.
  • CCHF Virus: Tick-borne, expanding range, ~30% fatality.
  • Orthopoxviruses: Monkeypox and future variants.

🔍 Key Pandemic Risk Factors

  • High human-to-human transmissibility (respiratory transmission).
  • RNA virus mutation rates enabling rapid adaptation.
  • Widespread zoonotic reservoirs (bats, birds, rodents).
  • Climate-driven vector expansion (mosquitoes, ticks).
  • Global travel, trade, and urban density.
